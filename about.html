Why do people come to counselling and therapy?

Psychotherapy and counseling are therapeutic approaches aimed at helping individuals overcome emotional, psychological, and behavioral challenges. They provide a safe and confidential space for clients to explore their thoughts, feelings, and life experiences with the guidance of a trained professional, such as a psychotherapist or counselor.

Psychotherapy involves in-depth discussions and exploration of the root causes of emotional distress, aiming to gain insight into one's thoughts and behaviors. It utilizes various evidence-based techniques tailored to the individual's needs to promote healing, personal growth, and positive change.

Counseling typically focuses on specific issues and challenges faced by clients, such as relationship problems, grief, stress, or career transitions. It can be more short-term and solution-oriented, helping clients develop coping strategies and practical skills to address their immediate concerns.

Both psychotherapy and counseling can benefit a wide range of individuals. They are valuable resources for those experiencing depression, anxiety, trauma, grief, substance use or addiction, and other mental health disorders. Additionally, they can support individuals dealing with life changes, relationship difficulties, low self-esteem, and personal growth challenges.

Psychotherapy and counseling can be particularly beneficial for individuals seeking to improve their overall well-being, emotional resilience, and self-awareness. These therapeutic approaches provide a supportive environment for clients to explore their thoughts and feelings, gain new perspectives, and develop healthier coping mechanisms, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling and meaningful life.

What kind of therapy do you practice?

I am a therapist who practices an integrative approach, drawing from a diverse range of therapeutic modalities to tailor treatment to each individual's unique needs. My approach encompasses neo-analytic techniques that delve into unconscious processes, existential exploration to address questions of meaning and purpose, cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) for addressing thought patterns and behaviors, and humanistic principles that emphasize personal growth and self-actualization.

I also incorporate person-centered methods to create a supportive and empathetic therapeutic relationship, as well as transactional analysis to examine interpersonal dynamics.

In addition, I utilize systems theory to understand how individuals operate within their broader social contexts, and acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT) to help clients embrace their emotions and values. Attachment theory guides my understanding of relationship patterns, while motivational enhancement techniques promote positive change and schema therapy helps to uncover and address long-standing patterns and beliefs.

Overall, my therapeutic approach is trauma informed, comprehensive and adaptable, drawing from these various theories to provide a holistic and effective treatment experience for each individual client.

Can I see you using a Mental Health Care Plan?

Being a full fee paying Counsellor and Psychotherapist enables me to offer more flexible, expansive and effective modes of therapy than what treatment under a Medicare Mental Health Care Plan can. Therefore I don’t provide rebated counselling sessions under a Mental Health Care Plan. As a member of the Australian Counselling Association (ACA), you may be eligible for a Private Health rebate with your provider.

What about my privacy and confidentiality?

Psychotherapists and Counsellors are bound by the Privacy Act to maintain confidentiality with clients UNLESS we believe you are going to harm yourself, someone else or abuse/harm a child. I’ll always discuss this with you before any action is taken.
